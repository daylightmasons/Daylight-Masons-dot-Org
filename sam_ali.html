Daylight‘s youngest elected officer, the number three officer in the Lodge, Sam Ali is a computer techie. He worked as a net work engineer when he joined the Lodge and is very representative of much of the Lodge‘s membership. He is a native of Tacoma born there September 9, 1981. He petitioned for membership after finding this web site and was Raised as a Master Mason by the Lodge‘s current Secretary, Anthony Monaco on April 21, 2007. He has proven to be an outstanding ritualist.

Some of the officers consider him to have one minor fault; he is a Washington State University Cougar. But that minor defect is overlooked because of the leadership he has shown since becoming an officer. He organized the Lodge‘s participation in the ROOTS program at the University Methodist Church where Daylighters cook breakfast two Sundays a month for homeless and need kids. He also organized a program of making contacts with University of Washington Fraternities and talking with fraternity members about Masonry. His commitment to being involved in community charities and doing something for those in need began at a very young age working with his family on Habitat for Humanity programs. This dedication to community service he has carried into his Masonic life always mindful of the Masonic charge that every person has a claim on his good offices; to do good unto all. He is a member of Oriental Daylight Chapter Royal Arch.
